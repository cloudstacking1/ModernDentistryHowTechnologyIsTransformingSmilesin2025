What is Dentistry?

Dentistry is a specialized field of medicine dedicated to the health and care of the teeth, gums, and entire oral cavity. It encompasses the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of a wide range of dental conditions, from cavities and gum disease to complex procedures like root canals, dental implants, and bone grafts. Modern dentistry goes beyond oral health it plays a key role in maintaining overall well-being, as issues in the mouth can affect the entire body. Whether through routine cleanings, cosmetic improvements, or restorative surgeries, dentistry is essential for ensuring a confident smile, comfortable eating and speaking, and a healthier life overall.

FAQ's

A dental bone graft is a surgical procedure where bone material is added to areas in the jaw that are deficient due to tooth loss, injury, or disease. This procedure is often necessary before placing dental implants to ensure there is sufficient bone structure to support them .

Initial healing from a bone graft typically takes about a week. However, for the graft to fully integrate with your natural bone, it can take anywhere from 3 to 12 months, depending on the size and location of the graft .

Most patients experience minimal discomfort during the bone grafting procedure, as local anesthesia or sedation is used. Post-operative pain is usually manageable with prescribed medications, and any swelling or bruising typically subsides within a few days .
